## Three Editions, Three Jobs

Each edition has a distinct identity, target customer, and job it does in the customer's world.

### Standard Edition
**Outcome:** "Service desk that just works"  
**Job:** Basic service delivery operations — request intake, queue management, simple routing  
**Target customer:** Small teams getting started with service management  
**Key focus:** Simplicity, speed to value, no-configuration workflows  

**What's included:**
- Request types and queues
- Basic reporting (OOTB dashboards)
- Forms and simple automation
- Service level targets
- Reporting templates

**Why customers buy:** Ease of use, quick setup, affordable entry point to service management

---

### Premium Edition
**Outcome:** "Orchestrate how work flows across your teams"  
**Job:** Structured ITSM and ESM across multiple domains and teams  
**Target customer:** Growing IT teams with formal ITSM practices  
**Key focus:** Workflow orchestration, organizational coordination, ITAM anchor

**What's included:**
- Change management (hard gate from Standard)
- Advanced automation and workflow builder
- ITAM (asset management, procurement integration)
- Multi-queue orchestration
- Advanced reporting and analytics
- Escalation policies
- Solution Composer (self-service authoring)

**Why customers buy:** Need for formal change control, multi-team coordination, asset governance

---

### Enterprise Edition
**Outcome:** "Govern service delivery at scale with confidence"  
**Job:** Proactive governance, compliance, and AI control at organizational scale  
**Target customer:** Large enterprises with compliance requirements, multi-team complexity, AI governance needs  
**Key focus:** Governance, compliance, organizational scale, AI control

**What's included:**
- Premium features + governance layer
- Multi-org support and org-wide analytics
- Enterprise SLAs and compliance controls
- AI Control Tower (governance, cost transparency, proactive analytics)
- Advanced audit and approval workflows
- Role-based access with fine-grained controls

**Why customers buy:** Compliance/audit mandates, multi-org complexity, AI cost control, large-scale analytics

## Landing Motions
Two distinct customer acquisition flows that determine initial edition placement:

### High-Touch (Sales-Led, ~68% of FY29 LRP revenue)
- Sales-driven qualification
- Deal desk involved
- Biases toward Premium and Enterprise
- Downsells to Standard when needed
- Complex, large organizations

### Low-Touch (AI-Led, self-serve)
- Product-driven onboarding
- In-app signal capture
- Automated routing logic
- Biases toward Premium with capability signaling
- Self-service teams

## Guiding Principles
Six principles underpin all edition positioning decisions:

1. **Edition identity is structural, not cosmetic.** Each edition solves a real, different job. Identity is backed by feature gating and pricing.
2. **Capabilities span editions, not stack vertically.** Each edition delivers the right experience for *its* job. A feature in Standard is simpler than the same feature in Premium, which is more structured than in Enterprise.
3. **The ladder is driven by usage signals, not sales pressure.** Edition movement (upgrades/downgrades) is governed by actual customer behavior and renewal cycles, not sales incentives.
4. **Competitive positioning is outcome-focused externally, maturity-focused internally.** Customers see outcomes (speed, orchestration, governance). We organize features by ITSM practice maturity.
5. **Pricing must reflect structural value differences, not just feature counts.** Standard→Premium is a ~2.5x price lift per agent. Premium→Enterprise is driven by seat growth (13x more agents typical).
6. **AI is a separate monetization surface, not bundled.** AI cost and governance are distinct from seat-based pricing.
