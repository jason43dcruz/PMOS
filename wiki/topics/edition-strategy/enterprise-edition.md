## Overview

**Positioning:** "Govern service delivery at scale with confidence"  
**Job:** Proactive governance, compliance, and AI control at organizational scale  
**Target customer:** Large enterprises with compliance requirements, multi-team complexity, AI governance needs  
**MRR (Jan 2026):** $22.1M (15% of JSM/ServCo MRR, growing at 90% YoY)  
**Paid seats:** ~588K (Jan 2026)  
**YoY growth:** +90% ($11.7M Jan 2025 → $22.1M Jan 2026)

Enterprise is the fastest-growing edition by percentage, but faces structural headwinds post-DC migration.

## Sales Motion & Customer Profile

### By Sales Motion
- **High Touch:** 92.5% of Enterprise seats (543,565 seats)
- **Low Touch:** 7.5% of Enterprise seats (43,980 seats)

Enterprise is overwhelmingly a high-touch sale. Low-touch contributes minimally.

### By Customer Size
- **Enterprise-segment (1000+ seats):** $21.4M (96.6% of Enterprise MRR)
- **SMB (< 1000 seats):** $756K (3.4% of Enterprise MRR)

Almost all Enterprise revenue is large organizations.

## The $23.3M Opportunity Question

**Critical finding:** Premium carries $23.3M in enterprise-segment MRR — **more than Enterprise itself ($21.4M)**.

These are large customers (1000+ seats) in high-touch selling that chose Premium over Enterprise. They are the primary upgrade opportunity.

### Why They Don't Upgrade

**Possible reasons from data and customer research:**

1. **Premium covers their needs.** ITAM, automation, and AIOps handle their core requirements without needing Enterprise governance
2. **Enterprise gates feel like checkboxes.** Governance and compliance matter but aren't creating sufficient urgency for upgrade
3. **No AI control story yet.** AI Control Tower is in EXPLORE. When it ships, it could be the structural reason to upgrade
4. **42.6% of existing Enterprise tenants don't use gated features.** If current Enterprise customers aren't using governance controls, compliance features, and org-wide analytics, prospects see even less reason to pay the premium

## Revenue Drivers

### Monthly Revenue Movement (Jul 2025 – Jan 2026)

| Driver | Pattern | Implication |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **New business** | Lumpy: $200K-$800K/month, $1.98M spike in Dec | Enterprise depends on a small number of large deals |
| **Expansion** | Steady: $264K-$1.26M/month | Customers land and add seats (healthy engine) |
| **Contraction** | Usually $100K-$200K; spiked to $439K in Jan | Watch for seat-cutting; Jan spike worth investigating |
| **Churn** | Very low, near $0/month most months | Enterprise customers rarely leave entirely |
| **Edition/pricing changes** | Modest: $114K-$204K/month upgrades | Premium→Enterprise upgrades happen but aren't the primary growth driver |

**Key insight:** Enterprise growth is driven by **expansion** (existing customers adding seats) more than new business. Large deals are lumpy.

## Why Customers Buy Enterprise

### From Customer Data (Gong, Sales Calls)

1. **Compliance and governance requirements** — 96.6% of Enterprise MRR is from enterprise-segment orgs with regulatory and audit mandates. These requirements don't go away.

2. **Multi-org scale** — 92.5% of Enterprise seats come through high-touch sales. Large, complex organizations need:
   - Multi-org support
   - Org-wide analytics
   - Enterprise SLAs
   - Centralized governance

3. **Seat growth after landing** — Expansion consistently adds $300K-$1.2M/month. Customers land with a team and grow across the organization.

4. **Analytics pull** — From Gong data: "I want it just for the analytics" is a stated Enterprise purchase driver. Org-wide analytics and reporting pull customers to Enterprise.

---

## The Structural Challenge — DC Migration Tailwind

**Finding from Supporting Data:**
~77% of Enterprise purchases are driven by **structural events** (DC migration, org consolidation) — not product feature-pull. Only ~23% cite Enterprise-specific features as the reason.

**Implication:** When DC migration completes (FY26), this tailwind disappears. **AI Control Tower becomes critical** — it's the strategic replacement reason for upgrading.

---

## AI as the New Structural Differentiator

### Current Blockers (Qualitative Research)
- **85 sales calls** cite credit/cost uncertainty as a barrier to Enterprise upgrade
- **30% of blocked deals** are waiting on org-level AI approval — not a product gap, a commercial/procurement friction
- **Predictable AI cost** is the emerging Enterprise pitch

### AI Control Tower Strategic Role
AI Control Tower delivers:
1. **Predictive cost modeling** — Org sees AI spend forecasts, controls budgets
2. **Org-level approval workflows** — Finance and security approve AI usage patterns
3. **Cross-tenant AI analytics** — See which teams are using AI, at what cost, for what outcomes

**Timing:** Ship AI Control Tower before DC migration tailwind fully disappears (FY26-27 boundary).

## Competitive Context

Vs. ServiceNow, Zendesk, Freshdesk:
- **ServiceNow Enterprise:** Feature-rich, but expensive and complex; compliance gates are structural
- **Zendesk Enterprise:** Compliance + advanced automation; governance is basic
- **Freshdesk Enterprise:** Compliance + custom integrations; limited org-wide analytics

**Our advantage:** AI Control Tower (when shipped) is differentiated. Governance infrastructure is table stakes.

**Our vulnerability:** 42.6% of Enterprise tenants have zero engagement with gated features. If they're not using it, why would prospects buy?

---

## Roadmap Implications

### Phase 1 (FY26)
- Fix Enterprise feature engagement (target: >60% actively using governance controls)
- Validate AI cost opacity as a real blocker
- Prepare AI Control Tower spec and sequencing

### Phase 2 (FY27)
- Ship AI Control Tower (multi-phase: cost modeling → approval workflows → analytics)
- Repricing when identity is real (governance + AI control as structural)
- Target Premium→Enterprise upgrades, especially $23.3M enterprise-segment cohort

### Phase 3 (FY28+)
- AI Control Tower fully mature
- Enterprise is the post-DC-migration governance tier
- Pricing reflects AI governance + org-scale value
