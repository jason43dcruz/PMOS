## Overview

**Positioning:** "Orchestrate how work flows across your teams"  
**Job:** Structured ITSM and ESM across multiple domains and teams  
**Target customer:** Growing IT teams with formal ITSM practices  
**MRR (Apr 2026):** ~$73M (56% of total JSM/ServCo MRR)  
**Paid seats:** ~1.26M (May 2026 snapshot)  

Premium is the "gravity center" of the business — the largest edition by MRR and the edition where most customer upside lives.

---

## What's Included

### Core Capabilities
- **Change Management** (hard gate from Standard) — Full change workflows with approval rules, impact analysis, rollback tracking
- **Advanced Automation** (soft-gated, expanded from Standard) — Complex rule builder, org-level automation, 50+ rules
- **ITAM** (hard gate from Standard) — Asset catalog, CI relationships, asset lifecycle workflows, procurement integration
- **Multi-queue Orchestration** — Cross-queue routing, escalation policies, SLA chains
- **Advanced Reporting** — Custom reports, trend analysis, team performance dashboards
- **Escalation Policies** — Smart escalation based on time, SLA, or custom rules
- **Solution Composer** — Self-service authoring, knowledge base creation
- **Workflow Builder** — Drag-and-drop workflow design, approval gates

### What's NOT Included (Hard Gates to Enterprise)
- **Multi-org Management** — No cross-org queues, org-wide analytics, centralized governance
- **Enterprise Governance Controls** — No fine-grained role-based access, audit trail, compliance controls
- **AI Control Tower** — No governance, cost transparency, proactive analytics for AI

## Pricing & Economics

**List price:** ~$50/agent/month (May 2026)  
**Actual average:** ~$37/agent/month (estimated, including discounts)  
**Price multiplier:** 2.5× Standard (~$20/agent)  

**Typical customer:**
- Multiple teams (50-500 agents)
- Formal ITSM practices (change management, asset tracking)
- Cross-team orchestration needs
- Sales-assisted onboarding (High Touch) or product-led (Low Touch)

## The Fragility Problem

**Edition mix challenge:** Premium is 56% of MRR but faces structural fragility.

### Activation Gap
- **Premium trial volume:** ~8× that of Standard
- **Premium trial conversion:** Only ~1/6th the rate of Standard
- **Root cause:** Customers are trying Premium features they don't need yet

### Feature Engagement Gap
- **37% of Premium tenants have zero engagement with gated Premium features** (change management, ITAM, automation beyond basics)
- **Implication:** Customers are paying for Premium but not using it — high downgrade risk

### Downgrade Risk
Premium tenants with low engagement are candidates for downgrade. If ITAM and Change Management aren't activated early, they'll de-value the edition.

## Upgrade Signals — Premium→Enterprise

From knowledge base (JSM Enterprise analysis):

### Enterprise-Segment MRR in Premium
**$23.3M in enterprise-segment MRR is sitting in Premium** — these are large customers (1000+ seats) who chose Premium over Enterprise. This is the primary upgrade opportunity.

### Why They Don't Upgrade to Enterprise

1. **Premium covers their needs.** ITAM, automation, and basic reporting handle what they care about
2. **Enterprise gates feel like checkboxes.** Governance and compliance matter but don't create urgency
3. **No AI control story yet.** AI Control Tower (strategic governance, cost tracking, proactive analytics for AI) is in EXPLORE. When shipped, it could create a new pull
4. **42.6% of existing Enterprise tenants don't use gated features** — if current Enterprise customers aren't using the gates, prospects see even less reason to buy

### Upgrade Path (When Triggered)

Monthly Premium→Enterprise upgrades average $114K-$204K (from expansion tracking):
- **New business:** ~$200-800K/month (lumpy, deal-dependent)
- **Expansion:** Customers land Premium, add seats, sometimes upgrade on renewal
- **Edition/pricing changes:** Track upgrades via this channel

## Standard→Premium Upgrade Drivers

From autoresearch and trial data:

### Primary Upgrade Drivers
1. **Change Management need** — Formal change control processes (most common)
2. **Multi-team orchestration** — Coordinating work across queues and teams
3. **Asset management** — Need for ITAM and procurement integration
4. **Advanced automation** — Hitting the 10-rule limit in Standard

### Signal-Based Opportunity
- **Addressable Standard tenants:** 3,782 (8.5% of 44,380 active Standard) with 2+ upgrade signals
- **Addressable MRR:** ~$75M (at list price)
- **Key signals:**
  - Queue config actions ≥5 (28d): 3.77× upgrade lift
  - Request type config ≥5 (28d): 2.96× upgrade lift
  - Active projects ≥3 + config actors ≥2: 3.53× upgrade lift

## Competitive Context

Vs. ServiceNow, Zendesk, Freshdesk:
- **ServiceNow Standard→Pro:** Adds Change Management, Reporting, Automation
- **Zendesk Professional:** Adds advanced routing, custom fields, basic automation
- **Freshdesk Biz:** Adds automation rules, SLA policies, advanced analytics

**Our advantage:** Premium feature density is comparable to competitors' mid-tier, but **we price 3-4× lower** than competitors at every tier.

**Our vulnerability:** Premium activation is low — customers aren't seeing the value they paid for.

---

## Roadmap Implications

### Phase 1 (FY26)
- Improve Premium onboarding (especially Change Management and ITAM activation)
- Measure engagement with gated features (target: >60% of Premium tenants actively using Change Management within 90 days)
- Don't change Premium pricing or features yet

### Phase 2 (FY27)
- Once identity is real (activation >60%), repricing to capture more value
- Differential pricing for Premium sub-editions (ITAM-focused vs. Change-focused)

### Phase 3 (FY28+)
- Post-DC-migration world
- Enterprise needs a new structural reason (AI Control Tower)
- Premium becomes the stable middle tier
