# Edition Strategy — Decisions Made

## What this page is

A running log of decisions that are **settled** in the edition strategy. Each decision is recorded with the reasoning and date. This prevents re-litigating closed questions.

---

## Settled Decisions

### 1. Standard is loaded — we accept this (Mar 2026)
**Decision:** We do not remove features from Standard to strengthen Premium. We explore price raises instead.
**Reasoning:** Six capabilities moved from Premium to Standard. Standard now includes AI, Assets, Automation. Customers can run a credible service desk on Standard indefinitely. But removing features would break trust and contradict PLG motion. Price raises are the lever.
**Source:** Layer 2 discussion, Anand alignment

### 2. No soft gates in the framework (Apr 8, 2026)
**Decision:** Every gate is either a hard gate (rock at one tier), layered rocks (distinct jobs per tier), or a meter. No "soft gates."
**Reasoning:** Every case examined (AI Control Tower, Analytics, On-call/incident) turned out to be either separate rocks at different tiers or a meter. Soft gates created confusion about what "partially available" meant.
**Source:** Session log Apr 8

### 3. Rocks get gated, not features (Apr 2026)
**Decision:** The unit of gating is the rock (value pillar), not individual features. Features are pebbles that travel with their rock.
**Reasoning:** Gating individual features creates a checklist mentality. Gating rocks creates a felt upgrade moment — "I need something I didn't have before."
**Source:** Layer 2–3 framework

### 4. Four editions is a constraint, not a choice (Apr 2026)
**Decision:** Free, Standard, Premium, Enterprise. Not 3, not 5. This is Atlassian global policy. Don't debate it.
**Reasoning:** Company-wide packaging architecture.
**Source:** P&P DACI

### 5. Single SKU (Service Collection) (Apr 2026)
**Decision:** CSM is bundled in Service Collection across all paid tiers. Can't gate separately.
**Reasoning:** Customer interviews validated that simplicity of portfolio is a competitive advantage over ServiceNow's modular pricing.
**Source:** P&P DACI, Monetisation hypotheses

### 6. UBP is an upgrade driver, not a revenue play (Apr 2026)
**Decision:** Consumption gates push customers toward the next edition. UBP targets <10% of revenue by FY29.
**Reasoning:** Monetising in-place through consumption undermines the edition upgrade motion.
**Source:** Monetisation hypotheses, R&I SVC Monetization

### 7. Default to Standard trials for LT (Apr 2026)
**Decision:** Low-touch customers should trial Standard, not Premium (reversing the current 10:1 Premium trial ratio).
**Reasoning:** Premium trials are 10× Standard trials at 1/6th the conversion rate. The math suggests Standard trials convert better.
**Source:** Layer 1 takeaways

## Things Explicitly Rejected

### "Collapse to two tiers"
**Rejected:** The data shows each tier serves a distinct buyer. Standard (PLG volume), Premium (mid-market operations), Enterprise (governance at scale). Collapsing loses the upgrade funnel.

### "Gate AI at Premium"
**Rejected:** ServCo includes AI agents at Standard. This is a competitive differentiator — ServiceNow charges consumption, Zendesk charges $50 add-on. Removing it would be a step backwards.

### "Add-ons for horizontal features"
**Rejected:** Customer research says add-ons only work for narrow vertical features. ITAM, AIOps, IT Ops must go through editions.
