## Edition Strategy

### 1. Three Editions, Three Jobs
Standard ("just works"), Premium ("orchestrate"), and Enterprise ("govern") are not price tiers — they're structural solutions to different jobs. Each has a distinct identity, target customer, and feature set. A customer in Standard isn't "lower" than one in Premium; they're solving a different problem with a different complexity level.

### 2. Premium is the Gravity Center and the Fragility Risk
Premium is 56% of MRR ($73M Apr 2026) with 1.26M seats. But 37% of Premium tenants have zero engagement with gated features (Change Management, ITAM, advanced automation). This is a structural risk: customers pay for Premium but don't use what they paid for, making them downgrade candidates.

### 3. Edition Mix Targets Are Real Constraints, Not Aspirations
LRP targets Standard 28%, Premium 56%, Enterprise 15% — and actual Apr 2026 mix matches almost exactly. Enterprise is growing 90% YoY but most of that is from DC migration tailwind (77% of Enterprise purchases), not product-pull. Once DC migration ends (FY27), Enterprise growth stalls without a new structural lever. AI Control Tower is that lever.

### 4. $23.3M in Enterprise-Segment Revenue Sits in Premium
Large customers (1000+ seats) with enterprise-segment buying profiles chose Premium over Enterprise. They see Premium as sufficient for their needs (ITAM, automation, basic reporting), and Enterprise's governance gates don't create urgency. This is the primary upgrade opportunity.

### 5. Upgrade Signals are Behavioral, Not Customer Attributes
Standard→Premium predictor: tenants doing ≥5 queue config actions in 28 days upgrade at 3.77× the rate of non-upgraders. This is measurable, addressable (3,782 tenants, ~$75M at list price), and actionable via targeted motion. We don't have equivalent Premium→Enterprise behavioral signals yet; current prediction relies on customer size + sales conversations.

### 6. Pricing is 3-4× Cheaper Than Competitors, But Multipliers are Steep
Standard $20, Premium $50 (2.5× multiplier), Enterprise ~$200 (4× from Premium). Competitors' Premium→Enterprise multiplier is 1.5-2×; ours is 4×. This creates upgrade friction at the top. AI Control Tower justifies the jump; without it, we need to reconsider the price or introduce a Premium+ step.

### 7. Feature Gating is Rocks and Pebbles, Not All Rocks
Hard gates (rocks) create upgrade boundaries: Change Management (Standard→Premium), Multi-org + Governance (Premium→Enterprise). Soft gates (pebbles) span editions with depth differences: automation (10 rules vs 50 vs unlimited), reporting (OOTB vs custom vs org-wide). Too many rocks and the ladder breaks; too many pebbles and the editions become indistinguishable.

### 8. DC Migration Tailwind is a Real Headwind
~77% of Enterprise purchases are driven by structural events (DC migration, org consolidation), not Enterprise feature-pull. Customers are consolidating infrastructure and forced to choose. Once DC migration completes (FY27), this driver disappears and Enterprise growth depends on product value, not infrastructure events. AI Control Tower timing is critical.

### 9. Standard→Premium Activation Gap is Real and Fixable
Premium trial volume is 8× that of Standard, but conversion is 1/6th the rate. Customers try Premium, find it too complex, abandon it. Fixing this requires: better onboarding for Change Management and ITAM, in-product education, measurable engagement targets (>60% using gated features within 90 days). Without this, signal-based targeting (the 3,782 ready cohort) won't drive conversion.

### 10. Premium→Enterprise Motion Requires New Structural Reason
Current Enterprise gates (governance, multi-org, compliance) are table stakes for large orgs but don't create upgrade pull when 42.6% of existing Enterprise tenants don't use them. Sales struggles to justify the 4× price jump. AI Control Tower (cost transparency, predictive modeling, org-level approval workflows) is the new structural reason — it creates a distinct value story for large orgs.

### 11. Repricing Must Follow Activation, Not Precede It
Don't reprice a tier until it has real, measurable identity. Premium pricing is justified once >60% of Premium tenants actively use gated features. Enterprise pricing is justified once AI Control Tower ships and adoption is clear. Repricing before activation is leaving money on the table and alienating customers.

### 12. JSM and ServCo are One Product, Two States
Migration mechanics are non-negotiable: no JSM customer sees a price increase, feature gaps must be eliminated, pricing must align post-migration. Edition strategy for ServCo is the same as JSM — same gating, same positioning, same economics. The constraint is migration velocity and customer continuity.

## AI PM Craft

### 1. Parallelism Beats Sequencing
When you have independent tasks (pulling data, reading a page, running a query), invoke them all at once instead of one-by-one. Expected speedup: 3-4×. Only sequence when task B needs output from task A.

### 2. Context Specificity Determines Output Quality
Vague prompts get vague outputs. Specific numbers, real customer data, exact failure modes, and clear constraints cut hallucination by 60-70%. The best prompts take 30 seconds to write and embed domain context upfront.

### 3. Sparring Requires a Draft First
Don't ask AI to build your strategy. Write it first (rough is fine). Then ask for sparring using the 10 moves (internal contradiction, alternate conclusion, data audit, source verification, unwritten assumption, misapplied evidence, adjacent implications, sequencing test, dangerous misread, constraint vs choice).

### 4. Strategy Sparring Surfaces Assumptions, Not Answers
AI sparring is pressure-testing, not decision-making. Use it to find the flaws in your logic, gaps in evidence, and hidden dependencies. But the decision is yours. If sparring reveals a real problem, go fix the data/strategy before finalizing.

### 5. Robotic Language Destroys Credibility
Kill AI intensifiers ("leverage," "empower," "synergy"), connectors ("Furthermore," "In summary"), adjectives ("comprehensive," "nuanced"), and filler ("Let's break this down"). Direct language (concrete verbs, specific numbers, short sentences) signals you've done the work. Readers trust direct language.

## Open Tensions

### Tension 1: Premium is Both a Landing Zone and a Destination
Standard→Premium upgrades happen (3,782 signal-ready tenants). But 56% of Standard tenants show zero upgrade signals and will stay in Standard forever. Premium is simultaneously "the next step up for ambitious teams" and "the actual destination for many customers." This creates messaging tension: do you pitch Premium as "the growth edition" or "the complete edition"?

### Tension 2: DC Migration Creates a Timing Cliff
Enterprise growth is 90% YoY because of DC migration tailwind (77% of purchases). But DC migration ends FY27. AI Control Tower is the replacement lever — but if it ships late or adoption is slow, Enterprise growth stalls hard at the cliff. Timing risk is real. We're planning for the cliff; the cliff is planning for us.

### Tension 3: Activation Requires Product Investment; Pricing Requires Customer Acceptance
Premium needs better onboarding to drive activation (>60% feature engagement). But customers expect pricing to stay flat while you improve activation. You can't say "We're repricing Premium because we finally got it right." You have to earn activation first, *then* price later. The window to reprice is narrow (18-24 months post-fix, before market resets expectations).

### Tension 4: Signal-Ready Cohort (3,782 Tenants) vs Actual Upgrade MRR
Signal analysis says $75M is addressable (3,782 Standard tenants with 2+ upgrade signals). But actual Standard→Premium upgrade MRR is much lower (~$1-2M/month). Conversion funnel is leaky. Question: is the signal weak, or is the motion weak? Probably both. Fixing motion without fixing the signal will disappoint.

### Tension 5: AI Governance is Strategic, But Timeline is Uncertain
AI Control Tower is the structural unlock for Premium→Enterprise (justifies 4× price jump, creates org-level value story). But it's in EXPLORE. Ship timeline is uncertain. Meanwhile, $23.3M of enterprise-segment revenue is in Premium waiting for a reason to upgrade. Can't use AI Control Tower as justification until it ships. What's the interim motion?
